Over the years, Kamelilo Day Secondary School has nurtured students who have gone on to achieve remarkable milestones in diverse fields. Our alumni continue to be a source of pride and inspiration, making impactful contributions in healthcare, technology, leadership, and social change. Here are just a few of their stories.

Elkanah Lemuya

Elkanah Lemuya, a proud alumnus of Kamelilo Day Secondary School, graduated in 2024 with an outstanding Grade A- (minus). His dedication, discipline, and passion for excellence made him one of the school’s top achievers that year.

Today, Elkanah is pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Nursing at Karatina University, where he continues to embody the values of hard work, compassion, and service instilled at Kamelilo. His journey is a true reflection of determination and the transformative power of education — proving that with focus and resilience, success is within reach.

Joseph Otieno

Joseph, Class of 2012, was always curious about technology. He developed his first mobile app while still in Form 3. After excelling in KCSE, he joined JKUAT, later founding a startup called 'J-Tech Hub', focused on youth tech empowerment. His story is a testament to the spirit of innovation cultivated at Kamelilo.

Mary Nduta

Mary completed her studies in 2008. She actively participated in debate and leadership clubs. After high school, she pursued international relations at USIU and earned a master's in diplomacy from Oxford. She now works at the UN in New York, advocating for African education reform policies.
